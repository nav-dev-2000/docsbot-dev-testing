# DocsBot product context for industry AI agent pages

Use this local context as the source of truth for DocsBot product claims. Do not use web search to invent or verify DocsBot product features, trust claims, reviews, or case studies. Web search is only for external industry research and quantifiable data.

## Core positioning

DocsBot helps businesses create AI agents trained on their own knowledge. Those agents can answer customer questions, support employees, retrieve information from approved sources, summarize context, identify knowledge gaps, and take bounded actions when configured through DocsBot AI Actions, Skills, APIs, connectors, or customer-defined workflows.

The product can be presented as an AI support agent, internal knowledge agent, documentation assistant, or workflow automation agent depending on the industry. Chat is one important interface, including embeddable website widgets and private team chat, but industry pages should not position DocsBot primarily as a generic chatbot.

## Customer support capabilities

- DocsBot creates AI support agents trained on help centers, product documentation, internal content, websites, team conversations, and other configured knowledge sources.
- Support agents deliver instant, source-grounded answers in the company's voice.
- Agents can work in a website or product chat widget and can support helpdesk-connected workflows.
- DocsBot agents can be configured to connect to, learn from, summarize, draft responses for, route, or answer support tickets in helpdesk software when the customer provides the right Skills, APIs, connectors, exported ticket sources, or approved workflow access. Do not claim native support for a named helpdesk unless it appears elsewhere in local context; describe this as helpdesk software, ticketing systems, Help Scout-style workflows, Zendesk-style workflows, Intercom-style workflows, or customer-configured helpdesk connections.
- DocsBot supports human escalation when a question is complex, sensitive, or needs a person.
- DocsBot can answer in more than 100 languages.
- DocsBot provides analytics, logs, topic management, reports, conversation review, satisfaction/resolution analysis, and knowledge-gap discovery.
- Customer support results used elsewhere on the site include millions of customer questions answered, automated conversation resolutions, conversations deflected, and hours saved.

## Internal knowledge capabilities

- DocsBot can act as a private internal knowledge assistant for employees and teams.
- It can train on internal documents, SOPs, policies, wikis, Google Drive, Notion, Confluence, PDFs, Word documents, text files, and other company knowledge sources.
- It gives employees instant answers about procedures, policies, tools, onboarding, troubleshooting, sales playbooks, brand guidelines, contracts, finance policies, compliance guidance, and operational processes.
- It can be used by HR, IT helpdesk, marketing, sales, engineering, operations, compliance, finance, legal, and other internal teams.
- Internal knowledge features include source-grounded answers, consistent information, always-available responses, onboarding support, reduced searching, and analytics around employee questions and documentation gaps.
- Internal knowledge evidence used on the site includes: 76% average resolution rate from DocsBot customer data, 3-6 hours per week often lost searching for information from Panopto/NewtonX research, nearly 2x readiness from exceptional onboarding from Gallup, 63% preference for preserved knowledge from Panopto, and 66% positive AI productivity effect from Gallup.

## Documentation assistant capabilities

- DocsBot can power documentation assistants for public or private docs.
- It can answer from technical documentation, developer docs, help articles, API references, source citations, tutorials, and knowledge bases.
- It helps users find answers without reading long documentation pages and helps teams see what users are trying to learn.
- It can surface knowledge gaps and repeated documentation questions.

## Presales and lead qualification capabilities

- DocsBot can support presales agents that answer product, service, pricing, compatibility, specification, availability, implementation, and fit questions from approved product pages, documentation, catalogs, PDFs, manuals, FAQs, policies, and sales enablement materials.
- Presales agents can collect lead details through a custom form or structured intake flow. Examples include name, email, company, role, region, timeline, budget range, project requirements, product interest, current system, and preferred follow-up channel.
- AI instructions can qualify leads by applying customer-defined rules, such as ICP fit, company size, urgency, product match, region, account type, use case, budget threshold, or readiness to book a meeting.
- With approved Skills, APIs, connectors, or workflows, a DocsBot agent can forward qualified leads, summaries, and conversation context to CRM, helpdesk, ticketing, forms, email, Slack, Microsoft Teams, or other sales systems configured by the customer.
- DocsBot can support booking flows by collecting scheduling intent and using customer-configured scheduling integrations or Skills for tools such as Calendly, Cal.com, TidyCal, and similar booking systems. Frame booking actions as approved, scoped workflows; humans should remain in control for complex pricing, contracts, regulated services, or high-impact commitments.
- Do not invent a specific native CRM, helpdesk, or scheduling integration. It is safe to describe customer-configured Skills, APIs, connectors, forms, webhooks, and workflow automations.

## Sales enablement capabilities

- DocsBot can support sales teams with internal sales enablement agents trained on product docs, service catalogs, pricing guidance, case studies, testimonials, competitive notes, objection handling, proposal templates, implementation notes, contract language, onboarding docs, and customer-facing FAQs.
- When configured with web search and approved Skills, agents can research prospects or accounts using public sources, summarize company context, identify likely pain points, and prepare sales-relevant talking points for a human seller.
- With approved Skills, APIs, connectors, or workflows, DocsBot can connect to sales software, CRMs, inboxes, calendars, enrichment tools, proposal tools, or helpdesk systems to retrieve context, draft follow-up emails, prepare responses, create tasks, update approved fields, and route handoffs.
- Sales enablement use cases should keep humans responsible for final outreach, pricing, legal terms, regulated advice, procurement claims, and contractual commitments.

## AI Actions and Skills capabilities

- DocsBot AI Actions extend agents beyond answers. Agents can answer, decide the next step, and take bounded action inside customer-defined rules.
- DocsBot Skills can give an agent reusable abilities with instructions, reference material, and optional executable functions for API calls, lookups, record updates, workflow triggers, summaries, generated artifacts, and other customer-configured actions.
- Skills can be used in customer-facing agents, helpdesk-connected agents, internal team agents, Slack, Microsoft Teams, and private chat experiences when configured.
- A DocsBot agent can use live system context when the customer has configured the right Skill, connector, API, metadata, or approved runtime binding.
- DocsBot supports MCP Connectors for connecting to remote Model Context Protocol (MCP) servers that expose tools from services a team already uses. Use MCP sparingly in generated copy, but include one natural mention for SEO when describing action/connectivity options.
- Appropriate AI Actions examples include looking up record status, creating a task or ticket, routing a request, collecting missing information, preparing a handoff, updating approved fields, generating a summary, notifying a team, or triggering a workflow.
- Additional action examples include qualifying a lead from AI instructions, submitting a custom lead form, forwarding a lead to CRM or helpdesk software, creating or updating a CRM record, drafting a sales email or helpdesk response, booking or requesting a meeting through a scheduling tool, researching a prospect with web search, and summarizing public account research for sales follow-up.
- Sensitive or high-impact actions can mention review paths, scoped permissions, logs, and customer-defined boundaries when that addresses a realistic buyer concern.
- Do not claim a specific third-party integration already exists unless it is in the local product context. It is safe to say Skills, APIs, connectors, or customer-configured workflows can support approved live lookups and actions where the customer has authorized the connection.

## Security, trust, and governance

- DocsBot offers SOC 2 Type II certification.
- DocsBot supports GDPR-ready governance.
- DocsBot uses encryption in transit and at rest.
- DocsBot supports granular access controls and role-based permissions.
- DocsBot does not use customer data to train public models.
- Skills are configured with environment values, secrets, metadata bindings, network policy, logging, and customer-defined controls so customers manage what agents can access and do.
- Credentials should stay out of chats and generated copy.
