## Overview
DocsBot should feel like a serious AI infrastructure product that is still approachable for support, operations, and documentation teams. The visual identity is crisp, practical, and product-led: dark cinematic marketing sections introduce the promise, while the app itself becomes quiet, dense, and task-focused.

The strongest brand signal is the cyan-to-teal action color. It appears in primary buttons, status chips, focus rings, animated call-to-action gradients, charts, and small icon tiles. The supporting system is mostly white, gray, slate, and black so the product feels technical and trustworthy rather than playful. Marketing pages often place this color over a dark navy canvas with a fine grid and a soft cyan glow.

Use two modes intentionally:

- **Marketing mode:** dark gray or near-black hero sections, oversized tight headlines, cyan/teal glows, gradient accents, and confident calls to action.
- **Product mode:** white cards on gray-50 backgrounds, compact controls, subtle borders, small typography, and sparse shadows for a dashboard that can be scanned for hours.

## Colors
The palette is a neutral SaaS system with a vivid cyan/teal core.

- **Primary cyan (#0E7490):** The accessible action color. Use it for primary buttons, active states, selected tabs, and key icon backgrounds.
- **Bright cyan (#0891B2):** The brighter product accent. Use it for gradient motion, glows, focus rings, badges, and large or bold CTA surfaces where contrast remains clear.
- **Teal (#14B8A6):** A supporting accent, most visible in marketing gradients and animated CTAs. Pair it with cyan rather than using it as a separate semantic color family.
- **Dark surface (#111827):** The marketing canvas. It gives hero sections, pricing headers, and dark feature panels a premium technical tone.
- **Hero grid and glow (#1F2937, #083344):** Use a subtle one-pixel grid and diffuse teal glow to make dark marketing sections feel technical without becoming visually noisy.
- **Light surfaces (#FFFFFF, #F9FAFB, #F3F4F6):** The app and content canvas. White cards sit on gray-50 page backgrounds; gray-100 is used for secondary panels, inactive controls, and grouped rows.
- **Text grays:** Gray-900 is the default text color, gray-600/500 are descriptive text, and gray-400 is for secondary icons and disabled hints.

Avoid broad rainbow palettes. Semantic colors should appear only for actual state: green for success, amber/yellow for warnings, and red for destructive or error states. Purple, blue, and pink may appear in integration illustrations or one-off diagrams, but they should not compete with cyan as the product accent.

## Typography
The primary type style is an Inter-first sans-serif stack with a neutral, modern voice. It should feel precise without looking sterile.

Marketing headlines are very large, semibold, tightly tracked, and often split across multiple lines. They rely on scale and contrast more than decoration. Body copy in marketing contexts uses medium weight and generous line height so it remains readable on dark backgrounds.

Dashboard typography is compact. Most operational text uses 12px, 14px, or 16px sizes, with semibold labels for controls and section headers. Reserve 24px and above for page-level titles, feature headers, pricing values, and marketing content.

Monospace appears sparingly for technical labels, API-like metadata, and uppercase eyebrow text. Use uppercase with wide letter spacing for small category markers, not for long prose.

## Layout
Layouts follow a practical 4px/8px rhythm. Product surfaces are compact and grid-like, while marketing sections use much larger vertical spacing.

Use a centered maximum width around 1280px for public pages. Horizontal page padding should be 16px on mobile, 24px on tablet, and 32px on desktop. Marketing sections typically use 64px to 128px vertical padding, while app panels use 16px to 32px spacing. Dark hero sections may use extra top space before the headline, especially when a global banner and navigation are present.

Dashboard screens should optimize for repeated use: persistent sidebars, top bars, bordered content regions, compact controls, and clear grouping. Do not create decorative cards inside cards. Use borders and section spacing to create structure before adding more shadows.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is restrained. Product UI uses borders first, then subtle shadows. A normal card usually needs only a gray border and `shadow-sm`; modals, menus, and overlays may use `shadow-lg` or `shadow-xl`.

Marketing pages can use more atmosphere: cyan glow shadows, dark radial or linear gradient accents, and animated gradient buttons. Keep these effects behind the message and do not let glow treatments reduce text contrast. Pricing and plan cards can lift more visibly with a dark outline or cyan-selected ring because they sit inside a dramatic dark section.

Glass and blur effects are acceptable only as light utility surfaces, such as a demo overlay or floating control, and should remain readable with solid text colors and clear borders.

## Shapes
The shape language is modern and moderate. The most common radius is 6px for buttons and form controls, 8px for app containers, and 12px to 16px for feature cards. Rounded-full is reserved for pills, avatars, toggles, and compact badges.

Large marketing visuals and pricing cards may use 24px to 32px radii, but the general system should not become bubbly. Sharp rectangular page bands and dark hero sections are part of the brand's more technical tone.

## Components
Primary buttons use cyan or the cyan/teal animated gradient, white text, semibold 14px type, a 6px radius, and a slight lift on hover. On dark backgrounds, focus rings should remain cyan with a dark offset.

Secondary buttons are white with gray text, subtle border or ring treatment, and a light gray hover. Ghost buttons on dark surfaces may use white text and a white translucent border.

Cards in the app are white, bordered gray-200, and lightly shadowed. Feature cards can use 16px radii and larger padding, but app configuration boxes should remain tighter and more utilitarian. Pricing cards are an exception: they are larger, rounder, and more presentation-oriented, with the selected plan using a cyan outline or glow.

Inputs are compact white fields with gray borders, 14px text, 6px radius, and cyan focus rings. Labels are small, semibold, and gray-700 or gray-900.

Badges and chips are pill-shaped. Use cyan-soft for informational and active states, gray-soft for neutral metadata, green-soft for success, amber-soft for warnings, and red-soft for errors.

Navigation should be calm and readable. Marketing navigation sits on the dark surface; app navigation uses gray and white with clear active states. Sidebar items should not be over-decorated.

## Do's and Don'ts
- Do use cyan as the dominant interaction color and teal as its gradient partner.
- Do keep app screens dense, bordered, and easy to scan.
- Do use dark hero sections for major marketing pages and pair them with oversized white headlines.
- Do keep shadows subtle in dashboard contexts.
- Do use semibold labels for controls, badges, and compact metadata.
- Don't introduce a new dominant accent color unless it represents a semantic state.
- Don't use large marketing typography inside compact dashboard panels.
- Don't stack cards inside cards when a border, divider, or section heading would clarify the layout.
- Don't use heavy blur or glow effects in the product dashboard.
- Don't make rounded corners so large that operational controls feel playful or soft.
